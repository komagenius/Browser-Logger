PURPOSE OF STUDY

The purpose of this research study is to investigate people’s understanding of expertise within the field of software engineering (SE). We wish to gain an understanding of SE expertise based on the perception of people who are associated with the field. The initial outline of the topics will act as a springboard for more detailed questions which will arise from participant’s responses.

SUBJECTS

Inclusion Requirements

You are eligible to participate in this study if you are at least 18 years old or older and have some knowledge of software engineering activities, work products and processes.

Exclusion Requirements

You are not eligible to participate in this study if you have no knowledge of software engineering or your age is less than 18 years old.

Number of Participants and Time Commitment

This study will include approximately 60 subjects and will involve approximately three hours of your time.

PROCEDURES

The following procedures will occur: In this study, you will be surveyed about your experience as a software engineer. This survey may be a written questionnaire or a verbal interview. The questionnaire may be administered on paper or over the Internet on a web site. The interview may be in person or over the telephone or by email. You will also be asked to answer questions that will assist researchers in evaluating their expertise. Questions will all pertain to the principal areas in Software Engineering (SE), namely: software development (requirements, design, coding) and social (people, groups, organization). You may be asked for examples of your work, including hard copy documents and computer files. The hard copy documents may include notes and reports. Computer files may include computer source code, design diagrams, project plans, testing plans, logs from tools, and requirements and specification documents.

RISKS AND DISCOMFORTS

This study involves no more than minimal risk. There are no known harms or discomforts associated with this study beyond those encountered in normal daily life.

BENEFITS

Subject Benefits

You will not directly benefit from participation in this study.

Benefits to Others or Society

There is no direct benefit anticipated for the subjects. The broader benefits to society include a better understanding of expertise that may be applied to the fields of education and computer science.

COMPENSATION, COSTS AND REIMBURSEMENT

Compensation for Participation

You will not be paid for your participation in this research study.

WITHDRAWAL OR TERMINATION FROM THE STUDY AND CONSEQUENCES

You are free to withdraw from this study at any time. If you decide to withdraw from this study you should notify the research team immediately. The research team may also end your participation in this study if you do not follow instructions, miss scheduled visits, or if your safety and welfare are at risk.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Subject Identifiable Data:

All identifiable information that will be collected about you will be removed at the end of data collection.

Data Storage

  • All research data will be maintained in a secure location at UCI. Only authorized individuals will have access to it.
  • All research data will be stored on a laptop computer that is password protected.
  • All research data will be stored electronically on a secure computer or network with password protection.
  • The audio/video recordings will also be stored in a secure location; then transcribed and erased at the end of the study.

Data Access

The research team, authorized UCI personnel, the study sponsor, and regulatory entities such as the Office of Human Research Protections (OHRP), may have access to your study records to protect your safety and welfare. Any information derived from this research project that personally identifies you will not be voluntarily released or disclosed by these entities without your separate consent, except as specifically required by law. Research records provided to authorized, non-UCI entities will not contain identifiable information about you. Publications and/or presentations that result from this study will not include identifiable information about you.

Data Retention

The researchers intend to keep the research data for approximately 5 years.
